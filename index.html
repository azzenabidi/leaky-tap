About the Establishment

A Tavern
Worth Finding

Tucked into the Eastern Outersteads of Zadash, The Leaky Tap has stood as a refuge for the city's working folk since before most can remember. Three stories of sturdy timber and stone, a raftered ceiling soaring twelve feet above your head, and room enough for a dozen tables — all filled, most nights, with honest folk seeking honest drink.

Don't come looking for gilded candelabras or velvet curtains. What you'll find here is something better: warm ale poured without judgment, a half-elf band that plays mostly on key, and a barkeep whose scarred smile tells you he's seen worse than whatever trouble you've walked in with.

Currently under the proud ownership of Lauren Schvine, the Leaky Tap carries on its legacy as Zadash's most reliable stop for bed, board, and belonging — no matter what empire says what on any given day.

The People Behind the Bar

Our
Crew

👩

Lauren Schvine

Proprietor & Owner

A Zadash local who took over the Tap from her friend Claudia Sheed. Lauren runs a tight ship with a warm heart. She knows this district, its people, and its whispers better than anyone. If you need something done in the Outersteads, she's likely the place to start.

🐉

Wessek the Trim

Head Barkeep

A plump white dragonborn with a scarred but jovial face — and the fastest hands behind any bar in the Outersteads. Wessek has a gift for opening tabs, remembering faces, and greeting even the most unusual parties with nothing but a raised eyebrow and a generous pour. "But there's six of you," he's been known to say. He'll find you a table.

"But there's six of you."
🎵

The House Band

Half-Elf Ensemble · Most Evenings

A rotating cast of half-elf musicians who've called the corner of the Leaky Tap home for longer than most staff can remember. They play folk ballads, working songs, and the occasional shanty from the Menagerie Coast. Mostly on key. Always full of heart.

Getting Here

Find the
Tap

We're in the Eastern Outersteads of Zadash — the part of the city the Crownsguard doesn't patrol as often as they should, which makes for a lively evening. Look for the sign with the dripping tankard. If you reach the Chained Alley, you've gone a touch too far.

No carriage service. No courier pigeons. Walk in, same as everyone else.
